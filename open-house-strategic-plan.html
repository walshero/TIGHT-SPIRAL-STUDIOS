Open House — Strategic Plan

Eleven loops ran. This plan is what they proved, and nothing it hasn't earned.

The bearing

Eleven delegates from ten domains trained on the core process for one month: play, notice, design. Each left with one documented loop at the smallest honest scale — already run, not planned. Governance was consensus above a hard-principle floor. The floors never moved; consensus roamed above them.

This plan follows the same physics. Every strategic move below is downstream of a proof. Where there is no proof, there is a parking spot, not a promise. Grant money, partners, and trust all follow evidence — so the plan compiles evidence.

What the loops proved — and the moves they unlock

Health care — the hospice intake

Proved: an intake can invite before it gathers. Scene-first and dignity-first survive the hardest room there is.

Move: the dignity-first floor is domain-portable. Offer the living-intake pattern as the studio's lead export to human-services partners.

Transit — one bus-stop sign

Proved: the legibility floor travels. Three stops, chosen by riders, on public infrastructure.

Move: feeds Eagle Eye's standing task — map TSP applicability across public infrastructure, one institution first. The sign is the pilot's pilot.

Museums — the one-object exhibit

Proved: the trust-then-turn mechanic (commit, then the record turns) works on an artwork as well as a president.

Move: the CYL trust-commit component graduates in the Props Room as a general-purpose mechanic, not a CYL part.

Agriculture education — the irrigation machine

Proved: kids notice the hidden real-world model before the reveal. Gee's open question leans toward noticed, not announced.

Move: Funny Boney's §5 transfer layer ships with the reveal late-and-quiet. Evidence for the Peter conversation.

Legal — the deposition-prep form

Proved: the "gathering this because" tag is a scalpel — two fields had no honest because, and they died.

Move: tagged-input becomes the studio's default form pattern; the playtest instrument is its first canonical carrier.

Music — the born-legal cue

Proved: a synthesized Web Audio asset carries full provenance in-file from the moment it exists.

Move: synthesized-in-file stays the preferred SFX lane; the provenance ledger cites this loop as its clean case.

Labor — the grievance meeting

Proved: the collision power-order de-escalates a live room. "Engagement proposes, floors dispose" is a governance tool, not studio trivia.

Move: the power-order gets a one-page public artifact — the consensus-above-the-floor explainer for partners and boards.

Libraries — storytime

Proved: scene-first survives a room of five-year-olds, which is the adversarial test.

Move: the scene-first Fidelity check stays binary and adversarial; no build gets a softer grader than the five-year-olds got.

Actuarial — the confidence line

Proved: a recommendation lands softer with its reason attached under the number.

Move: the confidence-call convention extends outward — every studio recommendation to a partner carries its number and its reason.

Fire service + flash fiction — the compressed drill

Proved: Sandbags' cut-as-ascent works on evacuation procedure — fewer words, higher ground, in both senses.

Move: compression-as-mechanic graduates from an EN195 game to a cross-domain training pattern in the Props Room.

The one promotion

The caucus promoted exactly one shared structure: a props exchange. One reusable component per domain per year, entering by the Props Room rules — recorded provenance, a why-held note, and an annual promote-or-kill review. Nothing accumulates by default. The exchange is a shelf, not a ball.

What we declined, on purpose

A motion for permanent chapters — a federation, letterhead, a network about the network — reached the table in week three. The room named it: katamari. Everything sticks, the ball grows, the bigness gets called progress. The motion was harvested for the props exchange and the rest was parked with a why-held note. The strategic plan's first strategy is the refusal: we scale by proofs, not by chapters.

Dissent preserved whole: two delegates hold that smallest-honest-scale underweights urgent domains. The objection lives in this record, unresolved, which is where it does its work.

Sequence

  • Now: ship the props exchange charter as one page. Promote the trust-commit and compression components already proven above.
  • Next opened: the public-infrastructure pilot (Eagle Eye's MassBay map) picks up the bus-stop proof as its first exhibit. The power-order explainer drafts when a partner conversation needs it — not before.
  • On evidence: a second open house happens only when the props exchange has survived one promote-or-kill cycle. The party must earn its sequel.
  • Never: chapters, federation, letterhead. Parked with the katamari note attached.

The walls

Floors carried unchanged through every domain, every delegate, every loop: dignity-first, scene-first, the legibility floor with its three visibly distinct stops, recorded provenance on every asset, FERPA-grade privacy, human-in-the-loop closing every generated thing, and the twin rule — adding requires pruning. Consensus governs everything above these walls and nothing below them.
