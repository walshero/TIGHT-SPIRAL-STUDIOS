# **WRITERLY MOVES — MASTER CANON**

Version: 1.0
Last Updated: June 2026
Status: Living Document

## **PROJECT DEFINITION**

Writerly Moves is a pedagogical framework that teaches writing as design through memorable metaphors, shared workshop language, playful revision practices, and interactive learning experiences.

The project exists across multiple formats:

- Physical card deck

- Workshop protocols

- Shared craft vocabulary

- Interactive game

- Facilitator guides

- Revision rituals

- Digital tools

The deck is not the product.

The deck is one expression of a larger system.

## **MISSION**

To make invisible writing processes visible, discussable, and playable.

Writerly Moves helps writers develop agency by naming the craft decisions that shape a reader's experience.

The goal is not formula.

The goal is flexibility.

## **CORE BELIEF**

Writing is not a set of rules.

Writing is a series of choices.

Writers learn best by:

- experimenting

- taking risks

- receiving feedback

- collaborating

- revising

- reflecting on their decisions

Drafts are prototypes.

Revision is playtesting.

Writers are designers.

## **CORE DESIGN PRINCIPLES**

### **Play supports deep learning.**

Writers need freedom to experiment, fail, try on new identities, and choose their level of effort.

### **Feedback should be amplified.**

Writers improve through conversation with peers, instructors, readers, and communities of practice.

### **Meaning is situated.**

Writing develops through authentic audiences, purposes, and contexts.

### **Choice increases engagement.**

There are multiple pathways to mastery.

### **Revision is discovery.**

Early drafts are prototypes rather than performances.

### **Metaphors are cognitive tools.**

Memorable images make abstract writing concepts easier to recognize, discuss, and revise.

### **Workshops should lower stakes while increasing agency.**

Psychological safety encourages experimentation.

### **Cards diagnose rather than prescribe.**

Writerly Moves identifies choices and possibilities rather than enforcing formulas.

## **EXPERIENCE DESIGN PRINCIPLES**

Every Writerly Moves experience should:

- Create a shared language for discussing craft.

- Encourage experimentation over correctness.

- Reward reflection rather than performance.

- Support multiple pathways to success.

- Emphasize iteration and revision.

- Treat writers as active designers.

- Promote collaboration and peer learning.

- Balance challenge with support.

- Be accessible to novice and experienced writers alike.

## **THE WRITERLY MOVES LOOP**

Notice → Name → Experiment → Receive Feedback → Revise → Reflect

All cards, workshops, games, and digital tools should reinforce this loop.

This framework derives from the iterative learning cycle:

Act → Reflect → Form a hypothesis → Test → Rethink.

## **FRAMING SYSTEMS**

Framing systems are overarching metaphors that organize multiple moves into memorable experiences.

Criteria:

- Memorable

- Playful

- Diagnostic

- Low-stakes

- Cross-card compatible

- Workshop-ready

### **Canonical Framing System: Drafty Air**

Core metaphor:

A draft is an aircraft preparing for takeoff.

Revision is a pre-flight inspection.

Functions:

- Lowers anxiety around revision.

- Creates a shared vocabulary.

- Bundles multiple moves into a coherent ritual.

- Normalizes iteration.

Associated moves:

- Character North Star

- Overpacked Suitcase

- Loose Threads

- Shaky World Rules

- Floating Camera

- Blurry Stakes

- Emotional Static

- Stock Footage Moment

- Fast-Forward

- Info Dump

- Convenient Coincidence
