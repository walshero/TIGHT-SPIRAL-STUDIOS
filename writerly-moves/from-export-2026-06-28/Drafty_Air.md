Drafty Air — Pre-Boarding Announcement

Good afternoon, writers. We’ll be boarding shortly. Before we do, please take a moment to look over your draft and stow any loose items.

–Your boarding pass is your Character Want, unique to each passenger. The story doesnt move without it. 

–Overpacked Suitcases should be consolidated. If a paragraph is carrying three outfits for a one-page trip, consider what really needs to come along.

–Please secure any Loose Threads. If you introduced an object, secret, or tension that asked for attention, make sure it hasn’t slipped under the seat.

–Be mindful of Shaky World Rules. Gravity, magic, memory, social norms; whatever governs your story should remain consistent throughout the flight.

–If you notice a Floating Camera, gently bring it back to your assigned seat. We should know where we are, who’s here, and whose eyes we’re looking through at all times.

–Blurry Stakes must be declared before takeoff. Readers need to know what the character wants and what turbulence looks like if they don’t get it.

–Please silence Emotional Static. Reactions should be tuned to the moment, neither overamplified nor mysteriously muted.

–Any Stock Footage Moments (generic images or default reactions) may be replaced with something more specific to this passenger, on this flight.

–If you’ve engaged Fast-Forward, be sure to slide past the boring bits, but  be sure not fly past the best views. Important turns deserve time at cruising altitude.

–Noticeable Info Dumps should be redistributed into carry-on details, ideally revealed in motion rather than announced over the intercom.

–Finally, check for Convenient Coincidences. If something arrived exactly when needed, please confirm it was properly seeded earlier or came at a cost.

Once everything is secured, you’re free to board.

Thank you for flying Drafty Air. We’ll see you in revision!
