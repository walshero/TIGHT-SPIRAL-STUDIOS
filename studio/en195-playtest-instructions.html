EN195 Playtest

How to play · How to log what you find

What you're doing

Play EN195 — The Last Week first. Everything else in the studio is in the cabinet — leave it closed for now. Your job isn't to grade the game. It's to notice where it works and where it fights you, and write those moments down as they happen.

How to play

  1. Open the arcade. Click the big lit tile: EN195 — The Last Week.
  2. Play it like a player, not a grader. Click things, wander, get lost, recover.
  3. Play a room or a chunk, then come log what stood out — the parts that snagged, fogged, or sparked you. Do this a few times as you go.
  4. If something big hits mid-play, jot it however you like — a phone note, a scrap — and add it to the log when you pause.
  5. One moment per entry. A single line is plenty.
The only thing that matters: log as you go, not all at the end. A few notes per session beats trying to remember everything at once.

The three kinds of moment

SnagSomething broke or blocked me. FogI was confused — didn't know what to do. SparkThis worked. Keep it.

What each log entry needs

Open the EN195 Playtest Log in Blackboard and add one entry per moment. Four things:

ScreenThe exact title text on screen when it happened. Copy it as you see it. What happenedOne line. Plain words. TypePick one: Snag, Fog, or Spark. Fix ideaOptional. Only if you have one.

Where it goes

Everything you log lands in the course Blackboard database and syncs to the gradebook automatically — you don't email anything or fill out a separate form. Your name and the time attach on their own. Just log what you honestly notice. A handful of short, specific notes helps more than one long paragraph — and there's no minimum. Whatever you catch is useful.
