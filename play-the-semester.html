Workshop 2 · Weeks 3–4 · June 8 – June 15

Poetry

Two poems, then the group. The goal isn't to decide what a poem "means" — it's to describe how it's landing.

Your job is to help the writer understand how their poem is being experienced by a thoughtful reader. — Poetry Workshop instructions

Workshop 3 · Weeks 4–5 · June 15 – June 24

The 10-Minute Play

A small stage, real tension. Write it, time it out loud, bring it to the group.

Happy protagonist + all desires fulfilled = boring. No tension. — Discussion Board #4 prompt
Week 4 · due Tue June 16
4.02–4.03 — Read for form and tension
The Play/Screenplay section of Chapter 10, plus Chapter 6: Tension. Start brainstorming your play.
Week 4 · due Wed June 17
4.06 — Read three plays
The Sculptor's Funeral, Going Viral, Family 2.0 — then Discussion Board #4 on what makes a ten-minute play compelling.
Week 4 · due Mon June 22
4.08 — Write your play
Original title, cast, setting, at-rise. 1,000–1,500 words. Do a timed read-through — aim for the 9–11 minute range. Then post for Workshop 3.
Week 5 · due Wed June 24
5.02 — Respond to your group
At least five margin comments plus a 100–200 word end summary on each play in your group.

Workshop 4 · Week 6 · June 29 – July 3 · you are here

Flash Fiction

The whole story in one breath — 750 to 2,000 words, self-contained, any genre. Then the final workshop, where you read in three roles.

You can draw generally from real life, but your work must be fiction, and it must be self-contained — no chapter 1, no to-be-continued. — assignment 6.03
Week 6 · due Mon June 29
6.03 — Write the flash story
750–2,000 words. Any genre, realism or speculative. Title it, share with comment access, post for Workshop 4 — then lurk until the comment deadline passes.
Week 6 · workshop
6.06 — Read in three roles
On each of your first three pieces, take a different reader role: the Compression Reader, the Image Reader, the Turn Reader. On the fourth, comment on the workshop itself.
Sandbags — cut it light
A baggy draft is the right problem. Tap words to cut; each one drops as ballast and the piece lifts. Practices the Compression Reader's move on your own draft.
