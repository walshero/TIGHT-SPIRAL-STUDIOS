# The Coil — how the Tight Spiral Papers braid with their exhibits
*Logged 2026-06-28 · Tight Spiral Studios · walshero@gmail.com*
*A TSP process spec. Captures the braided method so a fresh session resumes mid-coil, not cold.*

## The catch that named this
The prospectus assumed a one-way order: build the exhibit, then write the paper to frame it.
But Paper I got drafted around exhibits that aren't final — and that is not a mistake, it is
the actual shape of the work. Paper and exhibit are in a recursive loop. TSP does not go
paper-then-game or game-then-paper. It **braids**: each pass through one strand tightens the other.

## The rule (locked): the paper claim leads each coil
A coil always starts from what the paper *asserts*. The assertion becomes the test the exhibit
must pass. The exhibit is not the source of truth the paper reports on; the paper's claim is the
specification the exhibit is held to. This keeps the work honest — you cannot quietly lower the
claim to match a weak exhibit; you must either make the exhibit meet the claim or change the claim
on purpose, in writing.

## One coil = four beats (the studio loop, two strands)
1. **PLAY** — read the paper's current claim out loud. State it as a falsifiable promise about the
   exhibit ("a five-minute play teaches noticing the gap WITHOUT lecturing").
2. **NOTICE** — open the exhibit and test it against THAT claim, through the seats the claim
   summons (e.g. a "teaches without lecturing" claim summons the AI-Skeptic + Freire; an
   "accessible from the first screen" claim summons ADA + Visibility).
3. **REFINE** — fix the strand that failed. If the exhibit doesn't meet the claim, fix the exhibit.
   If the claim overstated what the exhibit can honestly do, change the claim — deliberately, logged.
   Only ONE strand is touched per coil (the other is the fixed reference for that pass).
4. **TIGHTEN** — adding requires pruning. A coil that only adds (more claim, more feature) is
   drift. Each coil names what it let you cut, fold, or settle. Cap: one substantive change per coil.

## The braid across the series
- Paper I's claim drives fixes to its two exhibits (CYL, the Auditor).
- A fix proven in an exhibit that recurs across papers graduates to the prospectus / OS (the
  Meta-Synthesis rule: a catch seen twice becomes a rule).
- Papers II–IV each open their own braid with their own exhibit, same rule: the claim leads.

## What is NOT a coil (the brake)
- Polishing an exhibit with no claim driving the change → that's optimization-before-implementation;
  PM SCOPE HALT.
- Rewriting the paper to sound better without a playtest behind the change → that's prose drift;
  the claim must be answerable by the exhibit.
- Running more than one coil per sitting when the maker is depleted → the Trauma-Informed seat's
  standing read: the braid is durable; the maker is the scarce, protectable resource.
