The iSLO Suite. Games that build the skill — and the instrument that measures it.

MassBay asks every graduate to become proficient in seven competencies. This is one station for each. Every station holds the games that build the skill by playing it, and the rubric that measures it against the college's own language.

Same skill, two instruments. And where the suite has no game yet for an outcome the college already measures, this page says so out loud — a dry cell, marked, not hidden.

How to read a station. On the left: the games. You build the skill by running into it — no tutorial, no account. On the right: the measure — the actual MassBay or AAC&U rubric, reproduced verbatim, the same instrument faculty use to score the graduation portfolio.

The rubric is ipsative by design. A starting point is never a verdict. It measures where a piece of work sits against the college's levels — and, played again, against your own earlier draft. It is not a ranking against your classmates.

Quantitative Skills

Graduates will use a variety of mathematical tools and quantitative reasoning to solve problems and analyze complex challenges.

Built

Build it — play these

The Tension Bar Client engineDecomposes a headline price into what it actually costs — a doorman is about $18,400 a year. The quantitative-reasoning engine the student build reuses. Measure it

Quantitative Literacy VALUE Not yet normed

The AAC&U Quantitative Literacy VALUE rubric is the field standard, but MassBay has not yet normed a local version. Proposed dimensions for the suite:

  • Interpretation — read a number in its context
  • Calculation — apply the right tool correctly
  • Assumptions — surface what the number hides
  • Communication — say what it means to a reader

Status: the build side now ships (Sticker Price); the measure side is still open. Norming a local QL rubric is a faculty task the suite can now support with real work samples.

Measure it

Tool-use & systems insight Suite-proposed

No standalone MassBay rubric is normed for this outcome. The suite proposes a criterion set that fits what these games actually exercise:

  • Fluency — operate the tool to reach a goal
  • Systems insight — explain how it works on you
  • Appropriate use — choose the right tool, and the ethics of it

Note: The Console is deliberately a dark-patterns game. Reading the manipulation is the appropriate-use criterion — you cannot use a tool well until you can see what it is doing.

4

Graduation Competency 4

Knowledge About the Natural World

Graduates will use scientific knowledge and methodology to test, validate, and update their knowledge about the natural world.

Dry cell

Build it — proposed

Update the Model ProposedYou hold a belief about how something in the natural world works. Evidence arrives. You decide whether to test it, what would change your mind, and how much to revise. The tight-spiral loop — hypothesis, test, update — applied to science. This is the studio's founding mechanic; no game has pointed it at Competency 4 yet.

Nearest existing relative: The Viscosity models a scientific framework (Maslow) as a space — the reveal pattern is proven, only the domain is missing.

Measure it

Scientific reasoning Not yet normed

MassBay has not normed a local rubric here. AAC&U's Inquiry & Analysis and Scientific Reasoning frameworks map cleanly onto the proposed mechanic:

  • Hypothesis — state a claim that could be wrong
  • Evidence — weigh what the data actually shows
  • Revision — update the belief, and say why

Status: the clearest gap in the suite. A build and a norming pass are both open — the strongest single opportunity for a new instrument.

Measure it

Responsibility & self-assessment Suite-proposed

No normed MassBay rubric. Because this outcome is largely self-reported, the suite proposes a reflective, ipsative instrument rather than a scored artifact:

  • Responsibility — own the choice and its result
  • Self-assessment — locate yourself honestly
  • Collaboration — contribute to, and credit, the group
  • Civic awareness — see past the self to the community

Fit: these games already produce the reflection; the measure is a rubric for reading it back, not a test. Dignity-first: a starting point is never a verdict.

How the suite is scored

The measure is not bolted on afterward. It is the same instrument the college already uses on the graduation portfolio — and it obeys the studio's floor.

Verbatim rubrics

Where MassBay has a normed rubric — Written Communication, ISLO #5 — it is reproduced word for word, attributed and dated. A criterion nobody can trace is a rumour.

Ipsative, not ranked

You are measured against the college's levels and your own earlier draft, never against a classmate. Criterion-based grading is what keeps minoritized students in the room.

The dry cells are named

Three outcomes the college measures have no game yet, and one live lane sits over a rubric still being built. The page shows the gaps instead of papering over them.

One engine, seven faces

Every station is the same move — make the invisible constraint visible, let the student find it. The rubric names what was found. The game is the practice; the rubric is the mirror.
