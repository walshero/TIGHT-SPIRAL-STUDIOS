Your RP World

Retinitis pigmentosa doesn't look the same to any two people. This is a short, private game about your world — what you notice, what's changed, what still works. Along the way you'll learn what RP actually is, and what's being studied right now.

Private and offline. Nothing you enter leaves this page unless you choose to send it.

What this is

Made by someone who lives it

This game is built by a teacher with retinitis pigmentosa. It has three parts: it maps your world (a few questions, no wrong answers), it reflects it back so you can see it named, and it shows what's being studied — with real links to find a trial. Your answers stay on this page. If you'd like to share them — to help design better tools — there's a button at the end that opens your own email. Nothing is sent without you.

Part 1 of 3 · your world · 1/5

Do you know your type?

RP comes from many different genetic causes. Many people — by some estimates up to two in five — never get a genetic diagnosis at all. That's not a gap in you. It's a gap in the science, and it's exactly what researchers want to understand better.

Part 1 of 3 · your world · 2/5

When did you first notice something?

Not when a doctor named it — when you first noticed. Trouble at dusk, bumping things at the edges, a night that got harder than it used to be.

Part 1 of 3 · your world · 3/5

What went first?

Most people with RP lose one thing before the others. In the research, nearly everyone names one of these. Which came first for you?

Part 1 of 3 · your world · 4/5

What's hardest in your day?

In the studies, two things get named more than anything else: getting around, and using screens. Which is heaviest for you right now?

And what still works?

RP takes things unevenly. Naming what's still strong isn't a consolation prize — it's real information, and it's how good tools get designed. What do you still trust?
