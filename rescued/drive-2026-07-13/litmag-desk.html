Government Cheese
submitted to the magazine

The block came in a brown box with no brand, just the word CHEESE in government letters. My mother cut it into squares the size of dominoes and called it a treat so we would believe her.

It melted wrong, in strings instead of pools, and it tasted like the inside of a thermos. I ate it every day for a year and never once said it was bad, because she was trying, for chrissake, and trying was the whole meal.

Years later I bought the expensive kind, the kind with a French name, and stood at the counter and could not make myself open it.
