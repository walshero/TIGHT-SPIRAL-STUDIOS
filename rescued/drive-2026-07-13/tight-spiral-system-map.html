The Review Panel — the governor

The panel isn't a gear; it's the governor on the train. It doesn't drive the work — it regulates whether the work is allowed to keep turning. Some seats hold a HALT (a hard brake: the AI-Skeptic, the Accessibility Officer, the Trauma-Informed Specialist, the Skeptic-Translator on presentation). Others are advisory — they add drag, not a stop.

You convene only the seats the job touches. A studio build pulls Craft seats; a classroom deliverable adds Classroom seats; governance pulls all three. The governor scales to the load.

The Opportunity Scout

Money sits here — and only here. The Scout watches for IP, value, and opportunity, but its gear is deliberately disengaged from the train. No money reasoning ever meshes with a design decision.

The wall is the feature. It only engages when the founder explicitly says: convene the Scout.

Three things the map makes honest

1. The hub is also an imprint. Human-in-the-Loop Productions is a named entity and the drive gear. It's not a sibling to the art studios — it's the center they mesh to. Every output carries a human gate because the gate is the hub.

2. The OS frames, the panel governs, the pipeline is the train. Only the studios are gears. Calling everything a gear would be a lie — if everything drives, nothing does.

3. Confluence is the instrument the hub reads by. The Measurement OS isn't a studio; it's the gauge mounted on the hub — it tells the human gate what the work is actually measuring, so the judgment is informed, not blind.
