Movement One · Headwaters

A conversation is a confluence.

Two people talked for an hour. The talk wandered, doubled back, drifted off and got pulled home again. Most of it would read like noise on a transcript.

But the studio has a lens. The same instrument that scores a student's comment, or weighs what a leader's words actually do — point it at the conversation itself, and something surfaces: the talk wasn't about the philosophy. It was performing it, live, while it happened.

This is the experiment. You'll run the lens by hand. You tag the threads; the tags compound; the pattern reveals itself. That move — notice, watch it converge, see the shape, go again — is the tight spiral. You're about to do one.

The six primitive lenses — your prism

A handful of simple parts. Like radicals that compound into a written character, these few refract across the whole talk and combine into larger meanings. Learn them once here; you'll use them to read the water.

Five short movements. No accounts, nothing saved, nothing sent.

Watch the threads meet.

Each thread you tagged is a tributary. Apart, they look like wandering talk. Drawn on one map, they pour into the same channel — and the channel has a name.

Each tributary carries its lens color and texture. Where they merge is what the conversation was really about all along.

Movement Four · The prism

One light, refracted six ways — then recombined.

This is the compound metaphor itself. The six lenses aren't six topics. They're refractions of one commitment, and they combine the way radicals combine: two simple parts making a meaning neither holds alone.

Movement Five · Downstream

What the lens revealed.

The conversation never explained the studio's philosophy. It enacted it. A friend gave one piece of contextual judgment a system could not generate — and in that moment became the first human in the loop. The talk drifted; it got caught and parked; the move was labeled out loud, never hidden. Every tributary you tagged poured into the same channel.

The lens didn't summarize the talk. It showed the talk was already the thing.

And that's the spiral

You noticed (you tagged). It converged (the map). You saw the shape (the prism). Now you'd go again — read a second conversation, and the lens is sharper because you've used it once. That recursion is Tight Spiral. You didn't read about the method. You ran one turn of it.
