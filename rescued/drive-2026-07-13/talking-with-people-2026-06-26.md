## PHILOSOPHY THROUGH-LINES (what's durable, widened to studio scope)

**1. Human-in-the-loop as a value, not a feature.**
The spine under every thread. The brain holds context and situated meaning the system can't; the human stays the principal, the system stays the tool that makes the human *better at their job* — never the replacement. "You're not out of a job, you're better at your job." Dan was named the first human-in-the-loop game designer for Choose Your Leader *because* he gave contextualized judgment the system couldn't generate.

**2. Label the moves; never hide the algorithm.**
The same ethic wearing different clothes across the whole catalog: Glass Engine shows the engine; the art imprint says "AI-assisted" out loud; the leader-scoring game would show *why* a score lands, not just the number. Transparency is the moral floor. "I hate this bullshit with hidden algorithms. So I label the move."

**3. The interview IS a studio method.**
What Matt did with Dan is repeatable intake for any discipline-specific build:
- "I have the context (the organization, the field-shape). You give me the brain."
- "Tell me what you need to do your job better."
- "What do people need to *learn* to be good at your job?"
- "Give me an anecdote I can learn from" (de-identified, compliant).
This is how a discipline-specific tool gets its task spine — from a practitioner's lived judgment, not from a content dump.

**4. A consensus-based, values-based profit model.**
Money reasoning stays walled off from design (the Scout rule already holds this). But the funding *philosophy* surfaced clearly: humans in the loop at all stages, paid; best people in each domain; transparency about AI use; redistribution-minded. Profit serves the values, never reshapes a build. Freedom of effort (one of the Four Freedoms) governs collaboration itself — commit what you can, full transparency, no guilt.

**5. The drift IS the method — when it's caught.**
Matt named his own pattern in the recording: the longer he goes from the original question, the more he drifts — like a long context window getting fuzzy. Coding/the OS is how he recognizes and rewires the pattern: "would you like me to put that in the parking lot and get back to business?" The parking lot is not storage; it's the discipline that lets the drift be generative without becoming a sink.

**(2) Choose Your Leader — scoring-layer design idea (NOT yet built, NOT locked):**
Score a leader by *who they actually help* and by *the emotional residue they leave.*
- **Help axis / "corruption meter":** does this actor help everyone / a minority / no one / only themselves-and-family? (self-and-family-only = near-zero.)
- **Delivery axis:** voter A supported this person to get A, B, C — did they get A, B, C, nothing, or the negative of it?
- **Emotional-residue axis:** does the leader's rhetoric leave shame / hate / guilt / confusion, or hope? (Ties directly to Dan's counseling insight that shame-and-guilt drive relapse — the same mechanism, read in public rhetoric.)
This feeds the existing CYL rubric/agent-pipeline work; it does not replace the seven binding conditions or the MVP-first rule (one rubric dimension, quote game, ships before visual/socioeconomic layers).
