Program Assessment Guide

What we assess, why it matters, and what you need to do this cycle.

What Is Program Assessment?

Program assessment is the department's systematic process for determining whether students are achieving the learning outcomes we've set for our courses. It is not an evaluation of individual instructors. Results are reported at the program level — no individual instructor's class is identified in reports.

MassBay is accredited by NECHE. Accreditation requires that we document student learning, review results, and use findings to inform curriculum. The English Department participates in a rotating annual cycle covering our core courses.

This year's assessment focuses on two courses and their primary writing outcomes:

EN101 — CLO 2: Argument EN102 — CLO 1: Analysis

EN101 CLO 2 — Argumentative Writing: Students develop and support a focused, evidence-based thesis in a sustained essay.

EN102 CLO 1 — Literary/Textual Analysis: Students analyze literary and expository texts using appropriate critical frameworks.

The signature assignments for each: EN101 Argumentative Essay (1,000–1,200 words); EN102 Literary Analysis Essay (1,200–1,500 words).

If you teach EN101 or EN102 this year:

  1. Complete the Norming Station before scoring signature assignments.
  2. Score the relevant signature assignment using the shared 4-point rubric.
  3. Select two student samples — one strong, one developing.
  4. De-identify each sample (remove student name, ID, and any identifying details).
  5. Submit via the Submit Sample tab by May 1.

If you teach EN095, EN195, or ESL courses, you are not in the assessment cycle this year but are welcome to use the norming and materials sections.
