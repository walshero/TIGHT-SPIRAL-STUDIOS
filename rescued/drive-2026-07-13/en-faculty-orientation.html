01 / Welcome

The Department at a Glance

The English Department at MassBay serves an open-access community college population. Our courses range from developmental and ESL instruction through college composition, literature, and creative writing. We are committed to equitable, evidence-based teaching and to preparing students for transfer and professional pathways.

This orientation walks you through the structures that guide our shared work: course-level outcomes, signature assignments, shared rubrics, and norming practices. Complete each section, then submit your responses to the Department Chair.

04 / Rubrics

Shared Rubric Models

The department uses shared rubric frameworks so that students in all sections receive consistent, fair feedback. Rubric language is descriptive, not punitive. You may supplement with course-specific criteria, but core dimensions are held in common.

Dimension 4 Proficient 3 Developing 2 Emerging 1 Beginning
Thesis Specific, arguable, forecasts structure Clear but underdeveloped or predictable Present but vague or descriptive Absent or merely announces topic
Evidence Credible, relevant, well-integrated with analysis Present but under-analyzed or loosely integrated Limited or summarized without analysis Missing, invented, or misused
Organization Logical, purposeful; transitions advance argument Generally clear; some abrupt transitions Discernible but uneven; ideas repeated or dropped Hard to follow; no clear structure
Conventions Consistent MLA; few errors that don't distract Mostly correct; some citation errors Errors begin to impede reading Pervasive errors; documentation absent
Dimension 4 Proficient 3 Developing 2 Emerging 1 Beginning
Image / Specificity Concrete, sensory details create vivid scene or moment Details present but uneven; some abstraction Relies on general language; telling over showing Abstract or clichéd throughout
Voice Distinct, consistent, and appropriate to form Identifiable but inconsistent in register Flat or imitates a generic "literary" voice Undifferentiated or uncontrolled
Structure / Form Deliberate choices in form serve the work's effect Form generally appropriate; some missed opportunities Default structure with little formal awareness Formless or arbitrary
Revision Draft shows meaningful transformation; reflection demonstrates insight Revision present but mostly surface-level Minor edits; little evidence of rethinking Essentially a first draft submitted
05 / Norming & Submit

Norming Exercise & Completion

Norming is how our department calibrates grading. Read the student excerpt below, score it using the argumentative rubric, and explain your reasoning. There are no trick questions here — the purpose is to surface assumptions and open a conversation before grades are submitted.

In today's society, social media has a big impact on people's lives. Many studies show that it can be bad for mental health, especially teenagers. According to one source, 40% of teens feel worse about themselves after using Instagram. This shows that social media is harmful and should be regulated. Some people might argue that social media helps people stay connected, but the negative effects outweigh the benefits. In conclusion, social media companies should be held responsible for the harm they cause.
