# Lumière Release + Student Permission Form
*Turns "I represent consent" into documented consent. Portable Markdown, HITL-owned by Matt. Draft — run the standalone form past the Chair/Provost (and ideally college counsel) before collecting signatures; the submission clause is lower-stakes and can go live with the next Lumière call.*
*Created 2026-07-13.*

## Part A — Lumière submission-terms clause (one line, add to the submission form)
Add to the Lumière submission page/form, above the submit button:

> By submitting, I grant Lumière and Tight Spiral the non-exclusive right to publish my accepted work and my name as author on Lumière and affiliated project pages (including archival and showcase use), and to reference it as a published/awarded work. I can withdraw future use in writing at any time. This covers my submitted creative work only — not my grades, assessment records, or coursework.

That single sentence makes every future Lumière author a clean `published-lumiere` / `public-award` basis in the manifest, automatically, at submission time — no chasing consent after the fact. This is the highest-ROI piece: it fixes the pipeline going forward with zero recurring work.

## Part B — Standalone permission form (for anything beyond published Lumière work)
Use this when you want to show a student's *classroom* work, writing sample, or any material tied to assessment. This is the form that actually satisfies FERPA's signed-consent requirement — it names the records, the purpose, and the recipient, and it's dated.

---

**Student Work Permission — Tight Spiral / Confluence**

Student name: _______________________  Course/term (if applicable): _____________

I give permission for the specific work described below to appear on public Tight Spiral / Confluence pages maintained by Prof. Matt Walsh.

- Work being shared (title/description): ______________________________________
- It may appear (check all that apply):
  - [ ] With my name as author
  - [ ] Anonymously / de-identified
  - [ ] As an excerpt only
  - [ ] Alongside a note that it is a course/assessment sample
- It may **NOT** include (default — leave checked unless you decide otherwise):
  - [x] My grades, scores, ISLO/assessment ratings, or any evaluation of my performance

I understand this is voluntary, does not affect my grade, and I can withdraw future use by emailing Prof. Walsh in writing.

Signature: _______________________  Date: ______________

---

## Part C — The two lines you cannot self-certify around
1. **Consent is the student's, not yours.** These forms exist because your authority as instructor isn't a substitute for the student's signature under FERPA. With the forms, you *have* their authority — documented.
2. **A public award never publishes a private score.** No release is needed to say "X won the CCHA essay prize" (public award). A release *is* needed to put X's name next to an assessment result — and even then, the Ledger gate stands: student assessment work is the Chair + Provost conversation first, never a live build.
