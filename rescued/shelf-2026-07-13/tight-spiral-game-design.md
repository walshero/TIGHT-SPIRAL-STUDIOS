# How a Tight Spiral Game Is Designed
*Standalone design doc · Tight Spiral Studios · walshero@gmail.com · 2026-06-30*
*Companion to the Studio OS and the Production Pipeline. The OS governs; the pipeline sequences; this doc states the design beliefs a build is judged against.*

---

## The one-line philosophy
**Play. Notice. Design.** A game teaches by having the player *do a discipline-real task* and *notice* something true, never by depositing a lesson. Grounded in Gee's tight-spiral loop, Osterweil's Four Freedoms, flow theory, and Nunan's task design.

---

## The two hard walls (never negotiable)
1. **No emoji, ever.**
2. **The safety walls** — trauma-informed (no ambush, no forced disclosure, always an exit) and FERPA (no real student data). These protect *other people*; they never become toggles.

## The accessibility floor (the founder's eyes)
Stated as a requirement, never as a look:
- **High contrast** for anything read; full-strength figure-on-ground; no muted-gray captions.
- **Large, clear type;** keyboard nav; visible focus; 44px+ targets; scroll-reset; phone-width; nothing hidden mid-screen.
- **Green-free in any primary or structural role** — green reads as mud (retinitis pigmentosa). State carried by position, label, shape — never green.
- **A reachable toggle** so other eyes tune contrast/size. The toggle *adapts*; it never gates content.

## The look is NOT the floor (corrected 2026-06-30)
The floor says what the eyes require and **nothing about what a build looks like.** Palette, composition, type personality, and overall look are decided **fresh per build and per screen** by the **Visual Designer, the Rhetorical Grammarian, and the Art Director**, driven by the **rhetorical situation of that specific game and that specific moment.** Reusing the last build's look "because it's the studio style" is a failure. **The standing brief is novelty and impact:** surprise the founder; aim for the look that makes him laugh or feel something. That felt response is the win condition.

---

## The required first beat
- **Scene-first:** every game opens by landing the player in a scene where they notice something. No text-first opening, no instructions/reassurance/setup paragraph before play.
- **Feeling-first:** the entry is proven to land *before* the mechanic behind it is built.

---

## The signature move: the Disciplinary Panel
Every game is *about* something a real scholar theorized. Before building, name the discipline and seat the minds who theorized it (4–8). Generative, not critical — it says what the game must *be*. Standing anchors: **Piaget** (build, don't transmit), **Freire** (problem-posing, reading word + world), **Saunders** (entry, withholding, escalation, sideways moral weight). Per-game bench extends these.

A founder lens underneath all of it: **Pedagogy of Real Talk** — start from real lived experience, not abstraction. The forbidden failure: *the spreadsheet of nouns* — a game that asks the player to rank or sort abstractions ("institutions," "the truth") has betrayed Real Talk. The player must *be someone real*, inside a specific lived moment.

---

## The task spine (what the player actually does)
Rank tasks by transfer: **Roles > Move-Things > Combo > Puzzles.** Locate Osterweil's Four Freedoms; **freedom to fail is non-negotiable** for a noticing game. The construct — what the build measures — is defined *before any content*, with computed evidence preferred over self-report.

---

## The house spines (the collections)
Builds group into collections sharing one felt spine (the player never reads its name):
- **Noticing** — read the word and the world; catch the gap. (Choose Your Leader, Sensory Memory.)
- **Calibration** — rehearse the scoring/norming move on neutral ground before real stakes. (Confluence, the calibration ladder, Nunan-task norming games.)
- **Show-the-Engine** — transparency; label the move, never hide the algorithm.

---

## The art system (three lanes, no default)
Run the **Medium Gate** every build. Never grind one medium toward what another does.
- **Lane A — Cut-paper SVG** (Tight Spiral Paper Craft): flat cut shapes, layering edge-shadow, matte grain, anti-mud palette. Load-bearing because it's skin-tone-neutral inclusive (the prop carries the role) and fully buildable offline.
- **Lane B — Raster / Lumino** (Super Sketchy Graphics, AI-assisted, transparently labeled): for photoreal handmade-craft looks SVG can't reach.
- **Lane C — Licensed photo/audio.**

## The provenance floor
No asset (art or sound) enters without recorded legal provenance — source, license, attribution, how it entered — captured before use. No scraping. Legal lanes: synthesized-in-file (Web Audio, CC0-by-construction) · CC0/public-domain · CC-BY (with auto-generated attribution) · own work · commissioned · licensed stock.

---

## The engagement seats (use the craft, never the trap)
- **Spend one affordance** per build (haptic, tilt, audio, gesture, motion) — or justify deliberate stillness.
- **Peak-End:** name the one peak the experience is built around; refuse a flat ending.
- **Variable-Reward Honesty (HALT):** use anticipation to deepen learning, never to manufacture compulsion.

---

## The persistence pattern: the Carry-Out
A single offline file can't store across sessions, so **emit-and-carry**: the game emits a record the player chooses to copy out — FERPA-clean, consent-first. Don't store; emit.

---

## The worked example: Choose Your Leader
Spine: *"You don't judge the leader, you judge what you were allowed to see."* A noticing game. Per-scene loop: scene-first onramp → blind-trust commit on the quote alone → the record turns (dated, sourced facts) → re-commit → the rhetoric pulls the player down a need-ladder; noticing the gap is the brake (computed from the trust-delta, never self-report). Bench: Freire spine, Saunders entry, Squire loop, Gee situated-meaning, Stuart Hall encoding/decoding core mechanic, McLuhan + Postman the medium, Arendt truth-and-politics reach. Three trauma-informed rails: consequence lands on the rhetoric's reach not the player's worth; the brake is always live; the deep ending teaches, never gotchas.
