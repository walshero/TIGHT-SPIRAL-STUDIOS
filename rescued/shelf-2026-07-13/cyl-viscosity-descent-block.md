# CYL Design Block — The Viscosity Descent
**Ruled: 2026-07-03, founder voice session. Supersedes open founder call #3 in `cyl-v5-rebuild-spec.md` (ladder vs. landscape vs. meters).**

## The ruling

The Maslow descent is not a HUD element, not a chart, not a poster. **It is the physics of the game world.**

- **World topology:** honeycomb. A central hub, areas radiating outward, short walks between them.
- **Maslow rung = global viscosity variable.** Every system reads it. As rhetoric pulls you down the ladder, the world thickens:
  - **Movement** — walks between hexes take longer, cost more
  - **Money** — saving gets structurally harder; costs and leaks multiply
  - **Creativity** — choice menus thin out; options that existed at rung 5 are simply absent at rung 2
  - **Hubs, walks, nerfs — all of it varies with rung**
- **Duration compounds.** The founder's framing: *"harder to move, save money, think creatively, while almost homeless for years."* The drag is not an event; it is a condition you live inside. Time at a low rung deepens the viscosity.

## The poster survives — diegetically

The Maslow ladder DOES appear as a poster — **on the wall in the school wing.** The player walks past the chart that names the mechanic the world is already enacting on them. That is the noticing move: the world is the meter; the poster is the label you can only read once you've felt it.

## Design consequences

1. **Founder call #3 is answered:** the descent's primary render is the world itself. Any marker/meter is secondary instrumentation at most.
2. **The three-way comparison build (ladder/landscape/meters) is retargeted:** what needs prototyping now is *felt viscosity* — one hub, two hexes, one walk, playable at rung 5 vs. rung 2, so the founder can feel the difference in his hands.
3. **Voice alignment:** moral weight through a concrete condition, never narrated. The game never says "you are struggling." The walk just got longer.

## Accessibility edge case — flagged per Maximize-or-check

"Harder to move" must be **structural friction, never input friction.** Viscosity is implemented as: more steps, longer paths, fewer options, higher costs, slower world responses. It is NEVER: sluggish controls, smaller targets, reduced contrast, degraded keyboard nav. All floors (44px, focus rings, keyboard, comfort stops) hold at every rung. The world resists the character; the interface never resists the player.

## C6 note

Viscosity needs zero instruction text. That is its whole virtue — the mechanic teaches by drag. Player-facing explanation stays under the 50-word ceiling trivially because the correct amount is zero.

## Next build target

**Viscosity slice:** one hub + two hexes + one connecting walk, toggle between rung 5 and rung 2, single-file offline HTML, founder plays both states back to back. Feel call happens in the hands, not on a scorecard.
