## 1. The Five PLOs — CANONICAL, VERBATIM
Source: `learningoutcomesassociatesartsliberalartsenglish2026.pdf` (Project shelf, AY '26–'27, verified 2026-07-02).

Graduates of the Liberal Arts: English concentration will have demonstrated the ability to:

1. Read and engage critically with a diverse range of texts and perspectives
2. Write using original thinking and voice to express a central idea, demonstrating purpose and audience awareness
3. Critically evaluate and integrate evidence to develop an argument
4. Demonstrate a collaborative, process-based approach to research, writing, and revision
5. Analyze the evolving roles that language and writing play in our identities, communities, and communications

Program framing (same source): the English Concentration serves students needing a strong background in writing/language arts — academic, creative, professional, and digital/multimodal writing. Degree awarded: Associate in Arts, Liberal Arts, English concentration.

## 2. Program structure (the course spine)
Source: `CurriculumSheetHumanitiesLiberalArtsEnglishAA.pdf` (Project shelf, AY '26–'27).

Required English sequence: **EN 101 → EN 102 → EN 120 (Intro to Digital Writing) → EN 202 (Advanced Writing)**, plus three English Concentration Electives (EN 195 Creative Writing, EN 210 Writing Short Fiction, or any college-level English/Communication/Literature course) and two Literature Electives.

Assessment implication: EN 202 is the terminal writing course — the natural exit-artifact site. EN 195 (Matt's course) is a concentration elective, a live artifact source.
