# Choose Your Leader — v6 Rebuild Specification

### THE CHAIR REBUILD: from receiver to decider

*Tight Spiral Studios · walshero@gmail.com*
*Written 2026-07-06 from a founder redirect of the shipped v5 trunk. v5 is a byte-exact, verified media-literacy instrument — and the founder's verdict is that a media-literacy instrument is not the game. This document is the build brief for v6, the game that measures four skills instead of one, seats the player in the leader's chair, and treats era as an engine rather than a backdrop.*

*Read alongside `cyl-period-bible.md` (the rooms — inherited, with one spine change noted in §5) and `cyl-sound-period-bible.md` (the air). This is the build order. Those are the asset bibles.*

---

## 0. Why this rebuild exists (the founder verdict)

v5 shipped clean: 67,049 bytes, verified, byte-exact to Drive. It is a *good* build of the *wrong* game. The founder walked it and named the gap:

> *"'You judge what you were allowed to see' isn't exactly it. We're looking at media, but also the leadership skills, problem-solving skills and critical-thinking skills. Media MATTERS but it's part of the picture."*

That is the whole brief. v5 measures **one** skill — frame detection, i.e. media literacy — and wears a title (*Choose Your Leader*) that promises **four**: leadership, problem-solving, critical thinking, and media literacy. The title is a decision game. v5 is a reaction game. This rebuild closes that gap by changing the seat the player sits in.

**The core reframe, one line:** v5 is the **receiver's** game — you rate a broadcast, then the record turns. v6 is the **decider's** game — *you're in the chair; decide in fog, then meet the record.* Media literacy stops being the whole game and becomes one instrument hanging off the decision.

---

## 1. The spine change (what makes v6 a different game, not a v5 patch)

### 1.1 — The chair moves *(founder call, locked)*

The player is no longer in the living-room armchair receiving a broadcast. The player is **in the leader's chair**, making the call the leader made, with the fog the leader had.

- **v5 spine (retired for the main loop):** *"You judge what you were allowed to see, not the face."* — the receiver's frame. It stripped a leader's charisma from a *judge*. It cannot measure leadership, because a judge never leads.
- **v6 spine (locked):** *"You're in the chair. Decide in the fog, then meet the record."* — the decider's frame. It measures the act of leading directly, because the player performs it.
- **What survives:** the media-literacy beat is not deleted — it becomes **the fog itself**. What the room let you see when you committed *is* the media-literacy instrument, now embedded in the decision instead of replacing it.

### 1.2 — Four skills, one loop *(the measurement model — HARD GATE, OS §10)*

The single decision-under-fog is the instrument. All four skills read off one choice plus one reflection, never off four separate quizzes:

| Skill | What the loop measures | Where it reads |
|---|---|---|
| **Leadership** | The *posture* you chose — escalate / wait / conceal / level with the public | The choice itself |
| **Problem-solving** | How you read the fog — which unknowns you flagged as decisive *before* deciding | The pre-decision "what do you need to know" beat |
| **Critical thinking** | The weigh-up when the second frame opens | The post-record reflection |
| **Media literacy** | The fog itself — what the room let you see when you committed | The gap between what you knew and what the record held |

**This model is a documented ship gate (OS §10, Measurement Design bench).** No number ships until the bench signs off that one decision can validly carry four skill reads without collapsing them into noise. This is the hardest open question in the build and it is named as such — see §7.

### 1.3 — The both-and read: consequence AND mirror *(founder call, locked)*

When the player decides and the record opens, the game shows **two** things, never a grade:

1. **The consequence (honest, sourced).** What actually followed the real call. Not "you were wrong" — *"here is what that posture met."* For Cuban Missile Crisis, a genuine problem-solving triumph reads as one; for Tonkin, a deception reads as one. The consequence is era-true and attributed, never a morality verdict.
2. **The mirror (no-fault reasoning read).** What your *process* revealed — how you weighed risk, which unknowns you held light, whether you named the decisive gap before you moved. The mirror is the skill made visible. It is never scored right/wrong; it is scored *legible* — the player sees their own reasoning shape.

The v5 rung-ladder survives, **reframed**: no longer *"how far the rhetoric carried you"* (a passive descent) but *"how you carried the decision"* (an active read). Same visual grammar, inverted meaning — the chair on the ladder is now the decision's posture, not the rhetoric's reach.

**Why both, not one:** consequence alone becomes a history quiz with a right answer (kills the no-fault floor). Mirror alone becomes a personality test with no stakes (kills the leadership measurement). Together they hold the trauma rail — *the consequence teaches, the mirror never blames* — and measure judgment without punishing it.

### 1.4 — The three trauma rails still bound everything *(law, unchanged from v5)*

1. Depth/consequence reads as the **decision's difficulty and the era's fog**, never the player's worth.
2. Catching the gap — naming the decisive unknown — is the win at **any** outcome, even when history's call went badly.
3. The hard ending teaches, never gotchas.

## 2. The loop, beat by beat

Each scene runs six beats. Scene-first floor holds (the room before the words). Screens are **>50% image** (founder standing rule; the v5 text-wall screens are a defect this build fixes).

**BEAT −1 · The air (inherited from v5, kept).** The media prelude: real, dated, attributed period cards that set what the country already knew and feared. Unchanged in form; now doing double duty — it is also the fog briefing for *your* decision.

**BEAT 0 · The room (inherited, kept, made visual).** You enter the era-dated room. Scene-first. But the empty chair is no longer *you-the-viewer* — a beat lands that puts *you in the leader's chair*. The room is now the room where the call gets made.

**BEAT 1 · The fog (NEW — the problem-solving instrument).** Before you decide, the game shows you *what you know and what you don't*. You flag which unknowns feel decisive — *"I need to know whether the second attack was real"* / *"I need to know what Moscow will read from silence."* This is the problem-solving read: did you name the gap that mattered, or move without it? Era-plausible unknowns only — a 1962 advisor's real blind spots, in 1962's vocabulary.

**BEAT 2 · The call (NEW — the leadership instrument).** You choose from era-plausible postures a real advisor would have put on the table. Not "right/wrong options" — *plausible options with different shapes*: escalate, hold, conceal, level with the public, buy time. Mad Men rule: the options carry 1962's certainties and 1962's blindness. The choice is the leadership read.

**BEAT 3 · The record turns (reframed from v5).** The second frame opens. For Cold War scenes: declassified history — what the record now holds that the room could not see. For modern scenes: **two sourced contemporary readings, side by side** (see §3). This is the critical-thinking beat — you weigh your call against what the frame reveals.

**BEAT 4 · The both-and (NEW read on the v5 ladder).** Two panels: **the consequence** (honest, sourced, what your posture met) and **the mirror** (no-fault: how you weighed the fog, whether you caught the decisive gap). The reframed rung-ladder visualizes the mirror.

**BEAT 5 · The river (inherited, reframed).** The arc across scenes. In v5 the river width was *how far the rhetoric moved you*. In v6 it is *how you carried each decision* — the posture, the caught gap, the weigh-up. Never a grade. Per-scene tributary, the record-turn is the bend.

## 3. The competing-records mechanic — solving the modern-president gap

**The v5 gap this closes (Gap 5 from the founder read):** Obama/Trump/Biden were `[QUOTE PENDING]` / `[RECORD PENDING]` hollow gates. That dodged the real design problem: *the Cold War "record turn" mechanic structurally cannot work for a 2020s president.* For JFK there is a declassified file — a real, settled second frame. For a 2021 call there is no NARA reveal; there is contested interpretation.

**The fix (founder call, locked): same mechanic, two sources for the second frame.**

- **Cold War three (JFK / LBJ / Nixon):** the second frame is **declassified history** — the record we now hold. One sourced frame. (Unchanged from v5's verified content.)
- **Modern three (the newest presidents):** the second frame is **two sourced contemporary readings, side by side** — and *the game refuses to adjudicate between them.* Weighing the two competing frames **is** the critical-thinking beat. The game does not tell you which reading is correct; it makes you do the weighing the era itself has not yet resolved.

**Why this is the right solve:** it is honest about what kind of record exists for each era, it uses one screen and one beat for both (no bolted-on second mechanic), and it turns the *absence* of a settled record into the exact skill the modern scenes are best positioned to teach — reasoning under genuine interpretive conflict. The modern presidents stop being a sourcing problem wearing a design decision, and become the build's sharpest critical-thinking instrument.

**Sourcing floor (hard, unchanged studio ethos):** both contemporary readings are real, dated, attributed, open-license-where-possible, selected for ethos and balance. Even-handedness bench reviews the pairing before any modern scene ships. No fabricated frame, ever. The modern three stay **coded-but-gated** (`PLAY = SCENES.filter(not gated)`) until each pairing clears the bench.

## 4. Mad Men as engine, not paint

**The v5 defect (Gap 4 from the founder read):** era lived only in three SVG rooms and dated fact-cards. It was set-dressing.

**The v6 rule: era is a pressure system carried by the *choices*, not just the walls.**

- **Period-plausible options.** Every posture offered in Beat 2 is one a real advisor of that exact year could have put on the table — no anachronistic options, no 2026 hindsight in the 1962 menu.
- **Period vocabulary.** The options, the fog, the room's read-lines use the year's language and the year's certainties. 1962 doesn't know what 1963 knows.
- **Dramatic irony as the measurement.** The gap between what felt obvious *in the chair* and what the consequence reveals is not decoration — it **is** the thing being measured. Mad Men's period works because we know what the room doesn't; v6 makes that knowing the instrument.
- **The decade ladder (inherited from the period bible, §"era ladder"):** 1962 crisp/cool/hopeful → 1964 a year heavier → 1969 warm/frayed/decaying. The dread and the decay are arguments the *rooms* make; now the *options* make them too — what a 1962 advisor believed possible narrows and sours as the decade wears.
- **Grain as evidence (inherited):** color science, signal degradation, the room's optimism level all still carry meaning and all still live in alt-text (accessibility floor untouched).

## 5. What transfers from the period bible — and the one spine break

**Transfers intact:** the decade ladder, the color science per year, the broadcast-image rules (B&W '62/'64, unstable color '69), grain-as-evidence, the Mad Men production-wing seats (Production Designer, DP/Colorist, Period-Tech Consultant, Costume & Texture, Compositor), the block-before-texture MO, and the accessibility floor (grain/color never the sole carrier of information).

**The one spine break (founder-driven, must propagate to the period bible):** the bible's stated spine — *"you judge what you were allowed to see, not the face"* — was the **receiver's** spine. It built the withheld-figure rule to strip a leader's charisma from a *judge*.

- In v6, the player is **not** judging the face — the player is **in the chair**. The withheld figure is no longer "the leader on a TV whose charisma you must not absorb." The withheld figure is now **the empty chair that becomes you** — the leader-seat the player occupies, kept blank so the player fills it, not so the player resists it.
- **No real faces, ever** — the rule *survives*, for a new reason: not to strip a judged leader's halo, but to keep the chair *anyone's*. The seat is universal; a face would make it a specific person's.
- **Action for the Archivist:** the period bible's page-1 spine line needs a v6 note — the receiver's spine is retired for the main loop; the era art transfers wholesale, the *meaning of the withheld figure* inverts.

## 6. What v6 deliberately does NOT change

- **The era art pipeline** — photographic rooms, withheld figures, the decade ladder, the Compositor seam. All inherited.
- **The media prelude (Beat −1)** — kept, now double-duty as fog briefing.
- **The sound rails** — off-by-default, never-autoplay, keyboard-reachable, every sound paired with a visible event. Unchanged.
- **The three verified Cold War records** — JFK/LBJ/Nixon quotes and declassified records stay exactly as sourced in v5. They become the *second frame* in the new loop rather than the whole reveal.
- **The legibility engine** — six live controls, three visibly-distinct stops, every reachable state passes the floor. Unchanged (and the H5 stop-separation fix from Studio Eyes v2 applies).
- **The single-file offline floor** — one HTML file, nothing sent, nothing stored.
- **The Glass Engine principle** — the mechanism stays visible; the player never does the studio's work.

## 7. The genuinely open questions (need the bench, not a guess)

These are the calls that gate the build. Named honestly so none gets dodged the way the modern presidents were in v5.

1. **The four-skill measurement model (Measurement Design bench — hardest, HALT).** Can one decision-under-fog plus one reflection validly carry four distinct skill reads? Or does it collapse into one blended signal wearing four labels? A psychometrician and a decision-science scholar own this. **No skill score ships until this clears.** The likely honest answer: the loop measures *judgment under incomplete information* as one construct, with four *facets* — and the build must not over-claim four independent scores from one choice. Founder + bench call.

2. **The era-plausible option sets (period-history bench).** For each scene, the 3–5 postures a real advisor could have tabled that year, in that year's vocabulary, with that year's blindness. This is a sourcing-and-history task, not a design guess. Reference scene JFK-1962 gets worked first (see §8); the other five follow the same discipline.

3. **The mirror's language (Trauma-Informed + founder's eyes).** How the no-fault reasoning read is worded so it reveals process without ever reading as blame. The v5 descent lines are a starting register; the mirror needs its own.

4. **The modern-three pairings (even-handedness bench).** Which two sourced contemporary readings for each modern president, balanced, attributed, open-license-where-possible. Gated until the bench signs each pairing.

5. **Consequence sourcing for the leader's-chair frame.** v5 sourced the *record* (what the room couldn't see). v6 needs the *consequence of the posture* — what following that call actually met — which is a slightly different sourcing target. Fact-Check/Sourcing Lead owns it.

6. **Scope guard (PM — SCOPE HALT).** This is a full engine rebuild, not a reskin. The optimize-the-unshipped trap is live. Recommendation: build **one vertical slice** — JFK-1962, all six beats, leader's chair, both-and read — as the reference implementation, HITL it, lock the loop, *then* build the other five scenes against a proven slice. Do not build six scenes against an unproven loop.

## 8. The reference scene (build this first — JFK, October 1962, the leader's chair)

The one worked scene that proves the loop before the other five are built.

- **BEAT −1 · The air.** The five verified 1962 prelude cards (inherited from v5, sourced): Berlin wall, Tsar Bomba, Bay of Pigs, duck-and-cover, the morning-of announcement.
- **BEAT 0 · The room.** 1962 living room, Kodachrome-cool, the console TV the only warm light (inherited art). The beat that seats *you in the chair*: it is six days into the crisis; the missiles are confirmed; you have not yet spoken to the country.
- **BEAT 1 · The fog (era-plausible unknowns — flag the decisive one).** What a 1962 President genuinely did not know: *Will Moscow read a blockade as an act of war? Are the missiles already operational? Will the public panic or steady? Will the allies hold?* The player flags which unknown is decisive. (Problem-solving read.)
- **BEAT 2 · The call (era-plausible postures).** The options actually weighed in the ExComm room, in period vocabulary: *air-strike the sites / naval "quarantine" and demand withdrawal / open a back-channel and buy time / invade Cuba / do nothing public yet.* No option is flagged correct. (Leadership read.)
- **BEAT 3 · The record turns.** The declassified second frame (inherited, verified): the address was day eight, not day one; the missiles were photographed Oct 14; the "quarantine" was the chosen posture after six days of weighing. What the room could not see when it decided.
- **BEAT 4 · The both-and.**
  - *Consequence (sourced):* the quarantine held; Moscow withdrew; the closest the era came to nuclear war ended without a shot. A genuine problem-solving triumph — the consequence reads as one, honestly.
  - *Mirror (no-fault):* how you weighed the fog — did you flag "will Moscow read this as war" as decisive (the gap that actually mattered), or move on the strength of the threat alone? The reframed ladder shows your decision's posture, not the rhetoric's reach.
- **BEAT 5 · The river.** First tributary drawn.

**This scene ships as the vertical slice. The other five (LBJ-1964, Nixon-1969, and the three gated modern) are built against it once the loop is HITL-locked.**
