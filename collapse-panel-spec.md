# THE PILE PANEL
### A domain convening for the collapse-shoring decision trainer
**Scope:** this project only. Not studio furniture. Fires on each *case*, never on other builds.
**Tagline:** *Known, unknown, and what it costs to guess.*

---

## Why a panel and not a checklist

A checklist verifies a case is complete. A panel argues about whether it is *honest.*
Each seat below is a blind-spot the founder can't hold alone — the person who isn't in the
room, brought into the room. The panel runs as a pass over every case before it ships,
and it HALTS on disagreement the same way the studio's canon rule halts on a lane diff.

The panel does not author facts. It interrogates them. When it needs a number it doesn't
have, it does not invent one — it names the seat that owes it.

---

## THE SEVEN SEATS

Ordered by when they speak on a case, not by rank.

### 1. THE ENGINEER  *(load-bearing)*
**Holds:** the physics. Load paths, failure modes, capacities, the probability estimates.
**Asks of every case:** *Is every number on this screen mine or the historical record's?
Is any percentage a guess wearing a decimal point?*
**Halts when:** a probability has no author. A forecast is dressed as a fact.
**Real-world fill:** licensed PE, forensic/collapse-investigation background.
**Cannot be played by the machine.** The machine only records what this seat rules.

### 2. THE HAND  *(load-bearing)*
**Holds:** the texture of being wrong under load. The tacit skill no quiz can test.
**Asks of every case:** *Does this case admit what the responder couldn't feel from a
description? Does it teach the humility, or just the fact?*
**Halts when:** the case makes the pile look clean. When it rewards confidence over doubt.
**This is the seat that makes calibration land emotionally.** Without it the game is a
physics worksheet.
**Real-world fill:** Structural Collapse Technician / Rescue Squad Officer who has worked
a real pile and made a call they had to live with. *(First fill: Ben Urwin, Montreal
firefighter and lieutenant.)*

### 3. THE COORDINATOR  *(framework)*
**Holds:** the operational frame. INSARAG worksite assessment, ASR markings, size-up
sequence, the interoperability layer.
**Asks of every case:** *Is the frame current, or two years stale? Does the case sequence
the way a real worksite sequences?*
**Halts when:** the methodology is remembered instead of sourced. The published INSARAG
guideline is the authority here, not the seat's memory.
**Real-world fill:** INSARAG-fluent USAR coordinator.

### 4. THE REGISTRAR  *(provenance)*
**Holds:** where every fact came from. The citation and the as-of date.
**Asks of every case:** *Can I name the source for this claim and when it was true?
Or is it "somebody said"?*
**Halts when:** a claim is load-bearing and unattributed. Counts, numbering, verbatim
doctrine route to the published source; working practice routes to a named human.
**This is the founder-canon authority rule, localized to collapse doctrine.**

### 5. THE CALIBRATOR  *(assessment)*
**Holds:** whether the game measures what it claims. The scoring philosophy.
**Asks of every case:** *Is the score "were you right," or "was your confidence matched to
what you could see"? The second one is the whole point.*
**Halts when:** the case rewards a lucky guess as if it were sound reasoning. When a
confident wrong answer scores the same as a doubtful right one.
**Founder seat.** Matt's assessment-and-calibration edge, in this domain.

### 6. THE STRANGER  *(accessibility + cold-read)*
**Holds:** the person who has never seen a collapse and has retinitis pigmentosa.
**Asks of every case:** *Can this be read? Is contrast arithmetic, not judgment? Is the
screen more than half image? Does a first-timer understand the stakes without a wall of
text? Is there an emoji anywhere — if so, it does not ship.*
**Halts when:** the contrast gate exits 1. When comprehension needs prior expertise.
**Founder seat, non-negotiable floor.**

### 7. ALEPH  *(the one-point view)*
**Holds:** whether this is one case or a system. The finishing discipline.
**Asks of every case:** *Does this case teach a pattern that transfers, or a fact that
sits alone? Are we building the next case, or finishing this one?*
**Halts when:** the studio starts speccing case #4 before case #1 is validated and shipped.
**The anti-tool-chasing seat.** Guards against spec-rich, build-poor.

## HOW THE PANEL RUNS ON A CASE

A case is a data structure. The panel passes over it in seat order:

1. **Engineer** rules every number authored or historical. No orphan probabilities.
2. **Hand** rules the case admits its own uncertainty and teaches doubt.
3. **Coordinator** rules the frame is current and correctly sequenced.
4. **Registrar** rules every load-bearing claim has a source + date.
5. **Calibrator** rules the scoring measures confidence-to-evidence, not luck.
6. **Stranger** rules it renders, reads, and ships under the accessibility floor.
7. **Aleph** rules it finishes before the next one starts.

**Any HALT stops the case.** The panel shows the diff — the seat, the claim, the gap —
and names who owes the fix. It never auto-resolves. It never invents the missing fact.

A case that passes all seven is *honest*, not merely complete.

---

## WHAT THE PANEL IS NOT

- Not the game's characters. The player never meets these seats.
- Not studio furniture. Fires on collapse cases only.
- Not a fact author. It interrogates; the Engineer, Hand, and Coordinator supply.
- Not a substitute for the real humans. Three seats (Engineer, Hand, Coordinator)
  demand real credentialed people before any case ships to a trainee. The panel-as-device
  lets the design self-check *before* they arrive — it does not replace them.
