# Decision — The Assignment Auditor as on-ramp / feeder to Confluence
*Logged 2026-06-28 · Tight Spiral Studios · walshero@gmail.com*

## The call
The Assignment Auditor is **the on-ramp and feeder to assessment**. Assessment itself
lives in **Confluence** (the rater-norming / calibration trunk). The Auditor is not a
rival system — it is the *intake river* that flows INTO Confluence.

## The shape (the founder's river concept, locked)
1. **Top level — follow the river.** A faculty member drops in an assignment. The river
   is the flow of their own logic: what it teaches -> how it's assessed -> which rubric.
   The three intake questions ARE the headwaters.
2. **Out the rings.** The artifact sits at the center; meaning radiates outward through
   the seven rings: artifact -> assignment -> course -> department -> program ->
   institution -> the outer free-thinking gear (universities reclaiming the space).
3. **Down for depth of application.** Below the rings, the Confluence depth descent:
   how the assessment actually applies, where the AAC&U dimension is really touched,
   what a rater would have to agree on. This is where it hands off to Confluence.

## Why it matters
- The Auditor catches alignment at the BEGINNING (intake), so a faculty member can
  fine-tune the assessment to get what they want before any student work is scored.
- Clean data in. No "dirty LLM" output — every move labeled, every stage gated by the
  panel (Trauma-Informed HALT informs every other seat), following the Tight Spiral OS
  end to end. The OS is the teaching point: the tool IS the method.
- It feeds Confluence: the Auditor normalizes the assignment so Confluence can norm the
  raters. On-ramp -> trunk.
