The workshop wall

Every move has a name and a hook.

You already used the first tool — the shears, when you cut the balloon light. Here's the rest of the rack. Each one is a thing on the wall: its label names the move, the object shows it, and the card flips a heavy line into a lean one so you can see the move work. Next stage, you pick one off the wall and use it on the piece that flew.
Tight Spiral Studios · walshero@gmail.com — The Workshop Wall, a labeled inventory of writerly moves for EN195. The bridge between cutting (Stage 1) and applying a named move (Stage 2). Single file, offline, nothing leaves the page. Examples are original studio lines. Green-free slate placeholder pending the palette pick.
How this was made: founder called for a Richard Scarry-style inventory — every move a labeled object. Mnemonic Iconographer held the HALT: each icon must beat its word to the eye. Studied Scarry's method (label-the-thing, busy-but-legible); no Scarry art reproduced.
