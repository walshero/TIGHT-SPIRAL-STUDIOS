On-ramp 1

The Playtest Platform

If you're in EN195 and you're here: welcome. There's no time limit and nothing to install. Open a game, poke around, and notice what the game makes you do — not just whether you liked it.

What helps most: where did you get stuck, what surprised you, and what did the game make you notice about writing? A sentence is plenty. Rough is fine.
See the games → Send a note about a game

The feedback link opens your email with a short template. Nothing is tracked or sent automatically.

On-ramp 2

The Assessment Depot

The studio's other half is calibration and assessment design — norming, scoring, rater training. The games are how that work gets delivered: a player sees the criteria, makes a judgment, and compares it to an anchor. That's a scoring session wearing a game's clothes.

If you're reviewing assessment, the Confluence console is the working example — the writing-competency scoring instrument built as an interface instead of a binder.

Open the Confluence console →
Reviewing this for a program? The scoring logic, the anchor set, and the score-to-outcome mapping are all visible in the console. Questions go to walshero@gmail.com.
