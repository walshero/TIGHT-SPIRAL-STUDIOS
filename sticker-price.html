The fine-print reading

Four headlines, four real numbers.

Here is what each sticker actually said once you did the arithmetic. Change any control and come back — the reading follows the numbers, not the other way around.

The one move, four times

  • Interpretation — the number you are shown is a claim, not a fact. Ask what it is the number of.
  • Calculation — do the arithmetic yourself. A loan, a total over time, a rate — the tools are simple; the discipline is using them before you decide.
  • Assumptions — find what the number left out. The denominator, the aid, the asterisk, the time.
  • Communication — say the real number plainly, to yourself and to someone else. That sentence is the deliverable.

How the arithmetic works, and where it comes from. The loan uses the standard fixed-rate amortization formula (monthly payment M = P·r ÷ (1 − (1+r)−n), r the monthly rate, n the number of months) — the same formula every lender uses. Net price (published cost minus gift aid) is the federal definition institutions must publish; see the U.S. Department of Education College Scorecard and the College Board's Trends in College Pricing. The statistic case is the base-rate / denominator problem from quantitative-literacy pedagogy. Every headline on this page is an editable example, not a claim about any real program or market — the numbers are yours to set. Method, not market. Verified against the formulas 2026-07-19.
