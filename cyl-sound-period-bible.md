# Choose Your Leader — The Sound Period Bible
### Sound direction for the composite room: era-dated broadcast audio + withheld voice

*Tight Spiral Studios · walshero@gmail.com*
*A production spec, not a built asset. None of the audio described here can be rendered in the offline sandbox — Web Audio synthesis makes tones, not a 1962 living room. These layers are produced in a capable lane (a sound designer, a properly licensed period-broadcast library, or an audio-capable session) and composited into `choose-your-leader-full.html`. The leader's voice stays non-impersonated in every case — the audio twin of the withheld-figure rule: you judge what you were allowed to hear, not a faked voice.*

*This is the audio companion to `cyl-period-bible.md`. Read them together — the visual bible dates the era with light; this one dates it with air.*

---

## The brief in one line

**The room hums the decade; the broadcast lands the exact year.** Before the player reads a single word, the *sound* should say which decade they are standing in — the weight of the room's quiet, the character of the set warming up — and the broadcast audio should say which year. The period is not the subject. It is the air, and air has a sound.

This is the audio half of the studio's first deliberate **composite build** (OS §3.2 per-layer Medium Gate; the Compositor seat, §5, owns the seam — now across sound as well as image). Two layers, two lanes, on purpose:

| Layer | Lane | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **The room tone + the broadcast** (set hum, the click of the set, the speaker's frequency limit, period reverb) | Produced sound — licensed period-broadcast library or a sound designer, attributed | Realism dates the era. A synthesized beep cannot carry the difference between a 1962 tube set and a 1969 one. This is the lane that makes the air real. |
| **The leader's voice** (the words on the broadcast) | **Withheld — never impersonated.** Treated as muffled broadcast presence, the *cadence and grain* of a televised address, never a recognizable vocal likeness | A faked voice reintroduces exactly the bias the game strips, the same way a real face would. The player hears *that someone is speaking with authority*, not *who*. |

**The seam is the meaning, heard.** A vivid, specific, dated *acoustic world* — and a deliberately withheld *voice* inside it. The room seduces the ear; the voice is held back. The Compositor's HALT now extends to sound: room tone, broadcast character, and voice treatment must reconcile so the seam reads as intention, never as an unfinished mix.

## The team in the room (who is directing this)

The *Mad Men* sound wing, seated as named roles. Each owns one lever of period audio rhetoric. (These mirror the visual bible's production wing — same logic, the ear instead of the eye.)

- **Supervising Sound Editor** — owns the whole mix: what the player hears first, what sits under, the balance between room and broadcast, the overall *quiet* of the piece. Holds the through-line.
- **Foley Artist** — owns the small real sounds of the room: the click of the set's power knob, the creak of the chair, the settle of the house at night. The handmade tells, in sound.
- **Broadcast-Audio Authenticity Consultant** — owns what a television of that exact year actually *sounded* like: the speaker's frequency range, the high flyback whine of a tube set, the bandwidth limit of the era's transmission. The audio twin of the visual Period-Tech Consultant.
- **Re-recording Mixer / Tone** — owns the room tone and reverb: the size and deadness of the space, the warmth or coldness of the air, how the decade's optimism reads as acoustic *roominess* or *closeness*.
- **The Compositor (Tight Spiral seat)** — owns the seam between produced room-sound and withheld voice; holds the HALT.

**The five period audio levers**, stated plainly so they can be directed without a studio: **room tone (the size and warmth of the quiet), the set's character (warm-up, hum, the speaker's frequency limit), broadcast bandwidth/fidelity, the reverb and air of the decade, and the voice treatment (withheld, never a likeness).**

## The era ladder (the through-line across three scenes)

The three Cold War scenes are **three different acoustic worlds**, not one room re-miked. The arc of the decade is an argument the game makes through the ear as much as the eye — and it should track the visual era ladder exactly:

- **1962 — clean, contained, hopeful.** A crisp tube set, a tight warm room tone, broadcast audio narrow but clear. The dread arrives *into* an orderly, quiet, believed-in soundscape — which is what makes it land. The high whine of the set is present but clean.
- **1964 — the same room, a year heavier, near midnight.** Quieter, later, the room tone a touch colder and emptier. The broadcast cuts *in* over late programming — a hard audio transition, a grave register. The air has thinned.
- **1969 — warm, frayed, unstable.** Softer, more degraded, the set's hum a little unsteady, the broadcast fidelity wavering the way early color wavered. The room tone is warmer but it reads as *tiredness*, not comfort. The signal feels less certain — the audio equivalent of the picture bleeding at the edges.

The player should *feel the decade pass* through the changing character of the room and the set, without being told a date.

## Scene 1 — JFK, October 22, 1962

**The room (Mixer / Tone).** A small, warm, contained living-room tone. Tight reverb — soft furnishings, low ceiling, the deadness of a 1962 interior. Beneath everything, the faint high whine of a tube television, clean and steady. The room is *quiet and orderly*, which is the point: the threat enters a soundscape that thought it was safe.

**The set + broadcast (Authenticity Consultant).** The click and warm-up of the set as it wakes. Then broadcast audio that is **narrow-band, slightly hot, contained** — the frequency range of 1962 transmission, a single small speaker. The broadcast is the only "loud" thing in the room, the way the screen was the only light. Clean, not degraded — 1962 is not yet frayed.

**The voice (withheld).** The address is present as the *cadence and weight* of a televised speaker — measured, grave, authoritative — but **never a JFK impersonation.** Treat it as the muffled presence of a broadcast voice: you hear that someone is speaking carefully to the whole country, not a recognizable likeness. Postman's note: it should sound *reassuring and in control*, so the gap lands harder when the record turns.

**Held breath (sound):** the room is almost silent before the set clicks on. *"It's late. Nobody's gone to bed."* — and the only sound is the house settling and the faint set-whine.

---

## Scene 2 — LBJ, August 4, 1964 (midnight)

**The room.** The same kind of room, **one year heavier and much later.** A colder, emptier room tone — fewer warm reflections, the deadness of a house near midnight. The faint set-whine is still there, a hair less steady.

**The set + broadcast.** The broadcast **interrupts** — a hard cut-in over the quiet of late programming, which itself should be barely-there low-level room sound that *stops.* The grave register lands stark against the empty room. Still narrow-band 1964 fidelity, colder than '62, more institutional.

**The voice (withheld).** A grave broadcast presence, still no likeness. The cadence of a leader reporting an attack — firm, certain. The certainty in the *delivery* is the rhetoric; the record will reveal the attack likely never happened. The voice must not telegraph that. Withheld, authoritative, certain.

**Held breath (sound):** *"Almost midnight. The regular program just stopped."* — the literal sound design: low late-night audio cuts to silence, then the cut-in.

---

## Scene 3 — Nixon, November 3, 1969

**The room.** **The hinge of the decade, heard.** A warmer but frayed room tone — softer, more degraded, the air gone a little slack. The set's hum is warmer and *unsteady* now, the physical wear of the late decade. Warmth that reads as tiredness, not comfort.

**The set + broadcast.** **The first uneasy fidelity.** By 1969 the broadcast can waver — the audio equivalent of early color bleeding: slight instability in level, a warmer but less-locked signal. The "direct appeal down the camera" means the voice sits *closer*, more intimate in the mix than the others — pulled toward the room.

**The voice (withheld).** A presence speaking *intimately to the room* — "to you, the great silent majority" — but still never a Nixon likeness. The withholding is hardest here because the rhetoric is most personal, which is exactly why the treatment matters most: the closeness is the seduction. Intimate, warm, withheld.

**Held breath (sound):** *"He's looking straight out of the set. Right at the room."* — the voice mixed closer and warmer than the room around it, as if addressed to you alone.

## Scene 4 — Obama, May 23, 2013 (the drone-war address)

*This scene is now live in the build (un-gated 2026-06-27). Its sound world is a different era again — the audio ladder extends past the Cold War trio into the modern broadcast.*

**The room.** A contemporary room tone — but the modern set is *silent* in a way the tube sets were not. No whine, no warm-up; a flat, clean digital quiet. The modern equivalent of the period tell: the absence of the hum is itself the date.

**The set + broadcast.** Full-fidelity modern broadcast — wide-band, clean, no bandwidth limit. The clip is the kind that will be "replayed and cut a hundred ways by morning" (the existing scene copy), so the sound design can suggest the *digital* nature of it — but never with alarm.

**The voice (withheld).** A measured, principled broadcast cadence — the *sound of restraint being set out loud* — and, as with every scene, **never an Obama impersonation.** The voice carries the authority of a leader holding power to account; the record reveals the operational gap behind the principle. The delivery must sound sincere and restrained, so the gap lands.

**Held breath (sound):** the flat modern quiet, then a clean clear voice — no warm-up, no whine, the eerie frictionlessness of the modern broadcast.

## The withheld-voice rule (applies to every scene — the Compositor's law)

The produced room-sound can be as real and specific as the lane allows. **The voice cannot.** Across all scenes:

- The leader is heard as **the character of a broadcast address** — cadence, weight, register, the grain of the era's transmission — **never as a recognizable vocal likeness, never an impersonation, never a deepfake, never a real archival clip of the actual person.** He is *the sound the era was allowed to hear*, rendered as exactly that and no more.
- The room is **you** — anyone, alone, at night, listening. Neutral, unspecific, never a named person's space.
- **No real voices, no impersonations, no voice cloning. Ever.** This is the exact audio parallel to the no-real-faces rule: a recognizable voice hands the player the charisma and the halo, which is the precise bias the game exists to strip. The same prohibition that keeps real faces out of the visual lane keeps real voices out of this one.

If the produced room and the withheld voice ever stop reconciling — the room one era, the voice from nowhere; the broadcast bandwidth not matching the set; the reverb of the voice not sitting in the room's space — the Compositor HALTs. The seam must read as *intention* (vivid acoustic world, withheld voice), never as an unfinished mix.

## The hard rails (law for the sound, from the panel)

These came out of the disciplinary panel convened on CYL audio (2026-06-27). They are not preferences.

1. **No sound dramatizes violence (Trauma-Informed HALT).** The LBJ and Nixon scenes are war; the Obama scene is civilian deaths. **No booms, no alarms, no tension-stings, no battlefield sound, ever.** Atmosphere stays in the *living room* — the set, the chair, the quiet. The violence lives in the dated record text, in plain language, never scored. This is the no-weapon-imagery voice rule, applied to the ear.
2. **Sound is off until enabled, and never autoplays (ADA HALT).** An obvious, keyboard-reachable toggle is the unlock. Web Audio and produced audio both require a user gesture; the toggle *is* that gesture.
3. **Every sound is paired with a visible event (ADA HALT).** The set-on click with the screen waking; the broadcast voice with the glow; any descent tone with the visual ladder drop. Sound is never the sole carrier of information. A player with sound off must lose *atmosphere*, never *meaning*.
4. **The entry stays small and real, not scored (Saunders).** A set-on click and the room's quiet — not a swell of dramatic atmosphere. The scene-first room opens in a held breath; the sound must not tip it into a movie.
5. **The broadcast seduces (Postman).** The room and voice should make the player trust the screen *a little more*, so the descent has something to push against. Do not telegraph the betrayal in the sound. Warm, reassuring, authoritative — then the record turns.

## Production notes (for the capable lane)

- **Lane & label.** Produced audio is credited per its source. If a licensed period-broadcast library is used, it is named and attributed (the sourcing ethos — named, verified, licensed, never a grabbed clip). If synthesized/designed, credited "Sound designed for Tight Spiral Studios." Never a real archival recording of the actual leader's voice.
- **No real likenesses, in voice or recording.** Produce *room tone and broadcast texture*, not impersonations. The voice is always the withheld treatment above. No voice cloning of any real person.
- **Sourcing honesty.** If a real period reference (a recording of a 1962 tube set's hum, era room tone) guides the work, it informs the *room*, never the person, and is attributed.
- **Block before texture.** Mirror the visual MO: first pass blocks the *structure* of the sound — where the set clicks, where the voice enters, where the room goes quiet — before any fidelity/period treatment. Never polish a mix that isn't blocked.
- **Composite handoff.** Each scene's audio is produced to the scene's beats, then wired into the existing shell behind the live interactive layer. The toggle, the visible-pairing, and the mute-kills-everything rule are built in the sandbox; the *sound itself* is dropped in from the capable lane.
- **The mute is total.** When sound is off, no oscillator drones, no room tone persists, nothing leaks. Tested on a real device.
- **Accessibility holds.** Room tone and broadcast character are atmosphere, never the sole carrier of information. Every rhetorical beat the sound carries also lives in the visible read-lines and the screen state. The Visibility floor is untouched.

## What the sandbox CAN build (the integration shell)

To be clear about the lane split, so this brief doesn't sit: the offline sandbox **can** build the *plumbing* now — the sound toggle (off by default, keyboard-reachable, the unlock gesture), the visible-pairing hooks at each beat (set-on, voice-enter, record-turn, descent), the total-mute guarantee, and a **synthesized temp track** clearly labeled as scratch (a placeholder hum and UI tones, never mistaken for the finished sound). The capable lane then **replaces the temp track** with the produced audio specified above — exactly as the raster art brief plans Lumino art to replace the cut-paper placeholders. The shell is sandbox work; the Mad Men treatment is capable-lane work.
