The whole semester, in one place you can play.

A syllabus can be a lot. I know my course schedule can be overwhelming. It's a lot of information, I know. My apologies. This is my attempt to do better.

A syllabus is a list. This is the same course as a place — a river you float down, four workshops along the banks, and a balloon you make rise by cutting the words that don't lift. Everything here is practice. Your real submissions live in Blackboard.

The compression climb · cut the dead weight

Make it fly.

A flash piece is a balloon carrying sandbags. Every word that doesn't lift is ballast. Tap a sandbag to cut it. Cut the dead weight and the balloon rises. Keep the lines that carry the story.

Tap a sandbag. The green ones lift — leave those. The tan ones are ballast.

It flew.

Here's the piece that was inside the heavy one the whole time — light enough to leave the ground:

She stood at the window with her coffee. The moving truck next door was already half unloaded — and the man carrying the boxes was wearing her dead husband's coat.

Compression: cut to what carries weight, keep what lifts. Implication does the work the cut words were doing. This is the Flash Fiction revision move — the portfolio piece is the one that flies.

The practice arcade · optional · extra credit

Three reader roles. Tap one to play.

Find the line doing the work of three.

One line is the real first sentence; the rest is wind-up. Which line could the story start on?

It was a cold morning, the kind that feels colder than it is. She had woken early. She made coffee and stood at the window. The moving truck was already next door, and the man unloading it was wearing her dead husband's coat.

Spot the abstraction with no picture under it.

Three put something in front of your eyes. One names a feeling instead of showing it. Which one blurs?

Which ending lands sideways on a gesture?

A good flash ending turns — it doesn't just halt. One closes on a concrete gesture that carries the story.

Where the river ends: the portfolio.
