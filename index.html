We build instruments that show you the rules while they are being run on you.

Most software hides its machinery. Ours hands it to you and lets you turn it. You pull the levers, then the seat turns around and shows you your own telemetry.

Some of these are games. Some are assessment tools sitting inside a college. One is running for a Manhattan relocation firm. They are the same engine wearing different clothes: make the invisible constraint visible, and let the person find it rather than be told.

LiveEducation lane

The bench

Every game here needs someone who has never seen it to walk in cold and get stuck in a way I did not predict. That is the whole job, and it is the most useful thing anyone can give this studio.

Sit the bench

You play one build. You get stuck, or bored, or you find the thing nobody meant to leave in. Then you tell me where it happened.

No experience needed. Not knowing what you are doing is the qualification — you are the only person who can see the game the way a stranger sees it, and you can only do it once.

How to playtest — the whole method, in four moves

  1. Play it cold. Do not read about it first. Do not ask me what it does. The confusion is the data — if you smooth it away by preparing, you have destroyed the thing I need.
  2. Notice the exact moment you stall. Not "it was confusing." "I clicked the amber bar and nothing happened and I did not know if I had broken it." The timestamp of your confusion is worth more than your opinion of the game.
  3. Say what you EXPECTED to happen. This is the one thing only you have. I already know what it does. I need to know what you thought it would do — the gap between those two is the bug.
  4. Tell me where you quit, and why. Boredom is a finding. Quitting is a finding. "I stopped because I did not care what happened next" is the most valuable sentence you can send me, and the one people are too polite to write.
What happens to this. It goes to me and to nobody else. No tracker fires when you load this page — this form is the only thing on this site that ever touches a network, and it only fires when you tap the button. How the studio works

Published, not because it is impressive, but because a shop that hides its method is asking you to trust it instead of check it.

Single file

Every build is one HTML file. Save it, mail it, put it on a stick. It has no dependencies and it cannot rot.

Offline

No external hosts. It runs on a plane, on campus wifi, in a building with no signal. Enforced by a check, not a promise.

Contrast is arithmetic

I have retinitis pigmentosa. Every text colour is computed against its background before anything ships. 4.5:1 or it does not go out. A gate refuses it on the server — not a person remembering.

No dark patterns

No streaks, no infinite scroll, no manufactured urgency. One game here is about those patterns. It would be strange to use them.

Scene first

No splash, no settings wall, no "choose your experience." The first thing you see is the thing itself.

Sources named

Where a build asserts a fact, it says where the fact came from and when it was checked. A number with no provenance is a rumour.
