What you just practiced

You felt a message move you, then checked it against what was really happening — and noticed the distance between the two. That distance is the whole skill. Not distrusting everyone; noticing when the words are reaching for you. You can do that with any speech, any ad, any headline, starting now.

What it watched

Two things: whether you noticed the gap between what was said and what was shown — and what it cost to trust before you saw the whole frame.

You noticed the gap in — of — moments. The more you caught, the higher you held.

How it's computed

It never asks you to rate yourself. It watches what you do: the distance between your blind trust and your trust after the record is the measure of gap-noticing. The cost lands last on purpose — you commit before you can see the frame, so the price is real.

Why you commit before you see the record

The game makes you rate your trust with only the quote in front of you — and locks it in — before it shows you the facts. That order is deliberate. If you saw the record first, you'd never feel the pull; you'd just grade the leader with hindsight.

Committing blind is what makes the gap real. The distance between your two answers is the whole measurement, and it only means something because the first one cost you something to give.
