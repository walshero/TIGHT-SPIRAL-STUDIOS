One grammar of motion, four moves, three grounds.

This page is a reference, not a game. Every future build pulls its easing from here instead of reinventing a curve per file — the way the contrast gate is one source of color truth, this is one source of motion truth.

Move 1 · Sisyphus letterhead — the studio signature. It rolls up, slips, begins again. Loops on --t-sisyphus.

The four moves

Settle-on-load

Content does not appear — it comes to rest. A short rise on --ease-settle, staggered by 80–120ms so a screen reads top-to-bottom the way an eye moves. That is the whole editorial voice: things settle, they do not pop.

Reveal

Delayed disclosure — the Console move. A control, then a considered pause, then the thing it hid. The pause is the meaning; instant reveal throws it away.

worst pair · 5.57:1 · AA

The reveal timing is --t-reveal on --ease-reveal — the same curve the telemetry screens use to hand you a number after you've committed to seeing it.

Move 3 · Comfort-stop crossfade

Tap a comfort stop, top-right.

The ground and ink cross-fade on --t-cross rather than snapping. Softer and Default are gate-certified. Warm-dark is hand-verified against the render-proof floor: the ground paints opaque so no white browser sheet shows through, and every glyph of text is cool near-white at full strength — amber lives only in the hill and the ground, never as a letter. That is the fix for the screen the gate once passed at 13:1 and a retina read at 1.17:1.

This whole page is the demonstration of move 3 — you are standing inside it.
