The Legibility Optimizer

Not a tone picker. It tunes the whole reading surface to your eye. Move a control — the text below changes live.

Tune the surface

Low-vision floor is 18–20px; 24px+ often preferred.

WCAG floor 1.5; low-vision comfort runs 1.5–2 to avoid crowding and find the next line.

WCAG spacing supports up to 0.12× font size; separates crowded letters.

60–80 characters reads most comfortably; shorter is easier to track back.

Even, moderate stroke reads best; very heavy can close up apertures.

Warm-dark is amber on charcoal — never pure black/white, which glares.

Read this at your own setting

The optimizer is not a comfort add-on. It is part of the accessibility floor. A reading surface that cannot be tuned to the individual eye is a surface that quietly fails some readers — measured in a hundredth of an em of letter-spacing, a few points of size, and a line-height value set once and never revisited.

Move the controls until this paragraph feels effortless to you. Your eyes are the verdict, not the numbers. When it reads without strain, that is the setting the studio should ship for you — and one day, adapt to automatically.
