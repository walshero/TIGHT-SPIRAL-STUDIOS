The Creative WritingArcade

Five machines that teach a writing move by making you do it. No lecture. No quiz. You play, and the craft is the mechanic.

Five cabinets · Free · Works offline
1

Cliché Cowpaths

Three cartridges · Field, Line, City

The cows never planned the route. They followed the cow in front. Three hundred years later the street still bends. A cliché is the same — nobody chose it, everybody walks it, and now it's paved.

Notice. The worn path under your sentence. Play
2

Sandbags

A flash fiction climb

The balloon won't rise. Every word you cut is ballast over the side. Cut too little and you never leave the ground. Cut the wrong thing and you crash.

Compress. Find what the story can't lose. Play
3

The Tell

An async workshop game

Every draft leaks. The writer knows what they meant; the page knows what they said. Read for the gap — the place the story tells on itself.

Read. What the draft admits by accident. Play
4

The Workshop Wall

Writerly moves, labeled

A move you can name is a move you can steal. The wall pins the craft where you can see it — and hands you the pin.

Name. The move, so you can use it. Play

The Review Bench

Flash fiction peer workshop

Praise is cheap and vague is worse. The bench makes you weigh a draft against something — and say what you'd trade.

Judge. With a reason you can defend. Play
